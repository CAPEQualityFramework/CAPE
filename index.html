CAPE pillars
The culture layer for everyday quality work

CAPE is not another project. It is a simple way to describe how your teams learn, own, and celebrate quality so that improvements move from a few champions to the whole unit.

C
Continual learning and improvement
Daily micro huddles, quick reflections on what worked today, and simple tests of change that staff help design and run.
A
Accountability with transparency
Whiteboard trackers, paper tallies, and peer check ins keep performance visible without turning conversations into blame.
P
Pride through recognition
CAPE Wins, patient stories, and leader rounding that highlight what teams did to move the needle, not just the final numbers.
E
Exceptional outcomes for every patient
Consistent use of evidence based bundles and celebrating zero harm streaks so reliability feels real, not aspirational.
How change moves
SIMPLE: the rule set for every intervention

Under CAPE, every change must be SIMPLE or it does not move forward. That is how we avoid initiative fatigue and still see movement on the measures that matter most.

S — Small
Sized to fit inside one shift or one huddle so teams can try it this week, not next quarter.
I — Intuitive
Clear at a glance. Staff understand what to do without a long in service or a new manual.
M — Measurable
Shows a simple before and after signal on paper tallies, run charts, or count boards that anyone can update.
P — Permeable
Easy to share across units with minimal adaptation so good ideas do not stay stuck in one hallway.
L — Low friction
Minimal added steps for frontline staff and aligned with the way work already flows on the unit.
E — Everyone embraces
Co designed with the people using it so it feels like their tool, not someone else's requirement.

How CAPE fits your current program

CAPE is intended to sit on top of the structures you already have in place and make them easier to see and feel at the unit level.

  • Frames daily and shift change huddles around learning, ownership, and recognition.
  • Uses whiteboards, clipboards, and simple counts to track key behaviors and outcomes.
  • Provides concrete stories and examples to bring to QAPI, safety, and board meetings.
  • Connects frontline efforts to the metrics leaders watch without promising new tech that is not there yet.
